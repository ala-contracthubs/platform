# Contract Hubs Platform Video Stack — Microsoft Teams (with POA as Exception)

Several V1 and post-V1 Modules will need to put two or more parties into a live video session — Legal consultations, Tenancy walkthroughs, future advisory calls, support sessions, and so on. The natural engineering default for sessions the platform itself creates is an embedded video SDK (Daily.co, Twilio Video, Agora) wired into our Case detail view, so participants stay inside our app and the Operator's actions happen next to the video.

We chose instead to make **Microsoft Teams the Contract Hubs platform-level video stack** for any session that *Contract Hubs creates*. Whenever a Module's workflow includes "platform initiates the video session," we will use Teams via the Microsoft Graph API: create the meeting programmatically, send branded invitations (where Teams supports branding), pull the recording and transcript back into the Case as documents. The platform pays a single licensing cost (per service account) instead of per-minute SDK fees, recording and transcription come in-box, and the tooling is already familiar to UAE business users.

**POA is the explicit exception.** The notarisation step in POA is not a session that Contract Hubs creates. It is a notarial session that the Notary office runs under the rules and tooling of the relevant UAE notarial authority (Dubai Courts, Ministry of Justice, or a private notary's own approved video system). The platform's job is to **relay** the join instructions the notary provides to the Client and the Agent — not to manufacture the video link itself. If the notary uses Teams, fine; if they use a court-mandated system, also fine; we don't dictate the stack and we don't host the meeting. This keeps us out of the regulatory line of fire on what a "valid notarial session" looks like and lets us onboard any compliant Notary office without forcing them onto our tenant.

The cost of this two-track approach is the inconsistency: a Client doing a Legal consultation enters a Contract-Hubs-branded Teams meeting; a Client doing a POA enters whatever the Notary office sends them. We accept that asymmetry because the alternative — forcing a single video stack across all Modules — either constrains POA partner selection (only Notary offices on our Teams tenant) or surrenders the platform's branding lever for the Modules where it actually matters (the non-POA ones).

## Consequences

- **For non-POA Modules that need video** (Legal, future advisory, future support), the Module Manifest declares `video_provision = platform_managed_teams`; the platform creates the Teams meeting on a Contract-Hubs-owned tenant (or the Module's nominated tenant), branded with our logo, recording and transcription on, and pulls outputs back into the Case via Graph API. The Module does not ship video code.
- **For POA**, the Module Manifest declares `video_provision = partner_provided`; no Teams creation, no Graph API integration on the POA side. The Case carries a `partner_video_join_url` (text) and `partner_video_instructions` (free text) that the Notary office's Operator populates before the appointment. The platform relays those values to the Client and Agent via Notification Center, email, and the Case detail view.
- **Notary office onboarding does not require a Microsoft 365 tenant binding.** The earlier draft of this ADR (POA-Teams version) required Notary offices to bind an M365 tenant; that requirement is dropped. Notary offices can use whatever notarial video system their authority approves.
- **Recording and transcript retention for POA are the Notary office's responsibility**, not the platform's. The platform's audit role on POA is limited to the Operator's attestation in the Session-Completion Checklist that the session was recorded per the notary's policy. If the Notary office wants to upload the recording to the Case for the Client to download, that's an optional manual upload via the standard documents primitive (Module 0 R5.1) — not a Graph API pull.
- **A future Module 0 amendment** should formalise `video_provision` as a Manifest-level enum (`platform_managed_teams` | `partner_provided` | `none`) so the contract is explicit and future Modules pick a side at registration.
- **Branding** is achievable only for `platform_managed_teams` (invitation card + lobby image, with Teams Premium). For `partner_provided`, the join experience is whatever the partner provides — no Contract Hubs branding.
- **Vendor lock-in surface is smaller** than the original ADR — Teams is now the platform's chosen stack for our sessions, not a per-Notary-office onboarding constraint. If we replace Teams later for the non-POA Modules, POA is untouched.
